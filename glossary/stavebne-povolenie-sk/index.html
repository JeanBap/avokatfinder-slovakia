Stavebné Povolenie

A stavebne povolenie is the building permit in Slovakia issued by the local building authority (stavebny urad) that authorises construction, extension, or significant alteration of a building before any qualifying works may commence.

Under Slovakia's Construction Act (Stavebny zakon), a stavebne povolenie (building permit) is required for the construction of new buildings, significant extensions, structural changes, and changes of use. The permit is issued by the local building authority (stavebny urad) operating within the municipality or regional office.

The application requires project documentation (projektova dokumentacia) prepared by a licensed designer, proof of land ownership, a land planning decision (rozhodnutie o umiestneni stavby) or confirmation of conformity with the local zoning plan (uzemny plan), and various specialist assessments where required.

Once granted, the stavebne povolenie is valid for two years within which construction must begin. The permit may be extended upon application. Construction carried out without a valid permit (cierna stavba — illegal construction) is subject to demolition orders and administrative fines from the State Building Supervision (Statna stavebna inspekcia).

At completion, the builder must obtain a kolaudacne rozhodnutie (completion decision / occupancy permit) confirming the works conform to the permit. Without this decision, the building cannot be legally occupied, connected to utilities, or registered in the Real Estate Cadastre as a distinct unit.

Buyers of existing properties should verify that all structures have valid permits and occupancy decisions. The local building authority holds records of issued permits. The Kataster entry also typically references the legal basis for the building. A property lawyer should flag any discrepancies between the physical building and its registered status.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers of rural properties sometimes accept that extensions were built 'without paperwork' as normal. In Slovakia, unpermitted extensions (additions without stavebne povolenie) are cierna stavby and carry demolition risk. Do not purchase without resolving the legal status of all structures.
💡 Expert TipAsk the local stavebny urad for a certified copy of all building permits and kolaudacne rozhodnutia for any property you are buying. This is a public record request. Cross-reference with the physical building to identify any additions not covered by the permit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a building permit required for internal renovation?

Generally not for works that do not affect load-bearing structure, facade, or technical networks. A simple construction notification (ohlasenie stavby) may suffice for intermediate works.

What is kolaudacne rozhodnutie?

The kolaudacne rozhodnutie (completion decision) is the Slovak occupancy permit issued after final inspection confirms the building conforms to the permit. It is required for legal occupancy.

Can I regularise a cierna stavba in Slovakia?

Slovak law has limited options for retrospective regularisation of illegal constructions. The current position is restrictive — consult a property lawyer on the specific facts before purchasing a property with unpermitted structures.
