Podielove Spoluvlastnictvo (Co-ownership)

Podielove spoluvlastnictvo is Slovak fractional co-ownership of real property. Common in inherited properties, it requires unanimous consent for major decisions including sale. Pre-emption rights between co-owners are mandatory.

Slovak law recognises two forms of collective property ownership: podielove spoluvlastnictvo (share co-ownership), where each co-owner holds a specified ideal share (podiel); and bezpodielove spoluvlastnictvo manzelov (BSM — community property of spouses), where married couples hold property jointly without defined shares.

In podielove spoluvlastnictvo, each co-owner may use the property proportionally to their share. Ordinary management decisions require a simple majority by shares; extraordinary decisions (sale, major renovation, mortgage of the whole property) require unanimous consent. A single dissenting co-owner can block the sale of the whole property indefinitely.

Slovak law imposes a mandatory predkupne pravo (pre-emption right) between co-owners: if one co-owner wishes to sell their share to an outside party, they must first offer it to all other co-owners at the same price and conditions. The co-owners have 60 days to exercise their right. Only if all decline can the share be sold externally. Violating this requirement can invalidate the transfer.

Court-ordered partition (zrusenie a vysporiadanie podieloveho spoluvlastnictva) is available to any co-owner who cannot agree on voluntary partition. The court will either divide the property physically (if divisible) or order a forced sale with proceeds distributed by share. The process can take years in contested cases.

BSM (community property) applies automatically to property acquired by married couples during marriage unless a pre-nuptial agreement (predmanzelska zmluva) specifies separate property. BSM shares are indefinite — they crystallise only on divorce or death.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Transferring a share in a Slovak co-owned property to an outside buyer without offering it first to all co-owners at the same price violates the mandatory predkupne pravo. The affected co-owners can challenge and potentially invalidate the transfer.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: For properties in BSM (marital community property), both spouses must sign the purchase agreement even if only one is actively negotiating. A sale by one spouse alone is invalid for BSM property.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if a property is in BSM?

Check Part B of the list vlastnictva — if the property shows two owners with the description 'v bezpodielovom spoluvlastnietve manzelov', it is BSM. Both names and signatures are required for any transfer.

Can co-owners sell to each other?

Yes — transfers between co-owners do not require the pre-emption procedure, as the right only applies to sales to external parties. Internal transfers also avoid Slovakia's minimal transaction costs.

What happens if a co-owner dies?

The deceased's share passes to their heirs through the dedic (inheritance) process. Heirs must register their inherited share in the kataster. This adds additional co-owners and can further complicate future sales.
