Dan z Prevodu Nehnutelnosti (No Transfer Tax in Slovakia)

Unlike most EU countries, Slovakia abolished its property transfer tax in 2005. There is no transfer tax on property purchases. Only VAT applies to new builds, and stamp fees are charged for kataster registration.

One of Slovakia's most buyer-friendly features is the absence of property transfer tax. Slovakia abolished its dan z prevodu nehnutelnosti (property transfer tax) in January 2005 as part of broader tax reform. Buyers of second-hand residential property pay no transfer tax whatsoever on the purchase price.

New builds sold by VAT-registered developers attract DPH (dan z pridanej hodnoty — VAT) at 20% on the purchase price. The VAT is typically included in the developer's listed price, but buyers should always confirm this. Reduced VAT rates may apply in specific social housing contexts.

The only transaction costs at the kataster stage are the navrh na vklad (registration application) fee — currently EUR 66 for standard residential transfers, reduced to EUR 33 for certain categories, with an expedited-processing surcharge of EUR 265. These are modest fixed fees, not percentage-based.

Annual property tax in Slovakia (dan z nehnutelnosti) is levied by municipalities on the assessed value of land and buildings. Rates are set locally and are generally low — typical annual bills for an urban apartment are EUR 50–200. The tax is assessed on the owner registered in the kataster on 1 January.

Capital gains tax on property (dan z kapitálovych ziskov) applies when a property is sold for more than its acquisition cost. The rate is 19% (or 25% for higher incomes). A full exemption applies if the property has been the seller's primary residence for at least five years or if the seller has owned the property for at least five years and it was their primary residence for most of that period.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Assuming no transaction costs because there is no transfer tax is incorrect. New builds attract 20% VAT, and legal and notarial fees still apply. Even for second-hand purchases, budget for EUR 300–800 in registration and professional fees.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: If you are planning to sell within five years of purchase, factor in the 19–25% capital gains tax. Waiting for the five-year primary-residence exemption to kick in could save tens of thousands of euros on a property that has appreciated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there really no Slovak property transfer tax?

Correct. Slovakia abolished it in 2005 and has not reintroduced it. This makes Slovakia one of the most transfer-tax-friendly property markets in the EU for buyers of second-hand residential property.

Does the five-year CGT exemption apply to investment properties?

No. The five-year exemption applies only to properties that were the seller's primary residence for most of the ownership period. Investment properties sold for profit are taxed at 19%/25% regardless of holding period.

What is the dan z nehnutelnosti?

The annual Slovak municipal property tax. It is split into a land component (dan z pozemkov) and a building component (dan zo stavieb). Rates vary by municipality. Owners must file a first-year declaration with the local municipality; subsequent years are assessed automatically.
