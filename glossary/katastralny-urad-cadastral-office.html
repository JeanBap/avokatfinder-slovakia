Katastrálny úrad (Cadastral Office)

The government office responsible for maintaining the official cadastral register of land parcels and ownership.

What Is Katastrálny úrad (Cadastral Office)?

The Slovak Cadastral Office, known as Katastrálny úrad, is the government agency responsible for creating, maintaining, and updating the cadastral register. This register contains detailed information about all land parcels in Slovakia, including their location, area, ownership, and any encumbrances. The office operates under the jurisdiction of the Ministry of Agriculture of the Slovak Republic.

Cadastral records in Slovakia include information about parcel boundaries, current owners, and registered encumbrances such as mortgages and easements. The cadastre provides the legal foundation for property rights and is essential for all real estate transactions. Information from the cadastral register is public and can be requested by anyone with a legitimate interest.

The Cadastral Office issues official cadastral extracts that serve as proof of ownership and property description. These extracts are essential documents in property transactions and mortgages. They provide reliable evidence of current ownership and any restrictions on the property.

Cadastral maps maintained by the office show the precise location and boundaries of each parcel. These maps have been digitized in recent years, making it easier to verify property information. Updates to the cadastral register are made continuously as property changes occur.

The Cadastral Office also handles applications for corrections to cadastral records when errors are discovered. Property owners can request corrections through an administrative process. These corrections ensure that the cadastral register accurately reflects actual ownership and property conditions.

Key Facts

  • Maintains the official cadastral register of all land parcels
  • Records ownership, boundaries, and encumbrances
  • Issues official cadastral extracts for property transactions
  • Operates digitized cadastral mapping system
  • Handles correction requests for cadastral errors

Common Mistake

Relying on informal records or oral agreements instead of official cadastral documents. In Slovakia, the Cadastral Office records are the legal authority for property information.

Expert Tip

When buying property in Slovakia, always obtain a current cadastral extract from the Cadastral Office. This simple step confirms ownership, reveals any encumbrances, and prevents purchase of property with hidden defects.

FAQs

How do I request a cadastral extract in Slovakia?

You can request one from the Cadastral Office online, by mail, or in person. The extract is issued promptly and contains official property information.

What information is shown on a cadastral extract?

The extract shows parcel identification, owner name, property description, area, and any registered encumbrances.

How often is the cadastral register updated?

The register is updated continuously as property transactions and changes occur and are officially recorded.
