Záložné Právo

Záložné právo is the Slovak term for a charge or lien over real property — a security interest registered in the Katastrálny úrad giving the lender priority rights over the property in the event of borrower default.

In Slovakia, záložné právo (pledge or lien) is the principal mechanism by which lenders secure mortgage loans against real property. The lien is registered in Section C of the list vlastníctva (title extract) and gives the registered lender the right to enforce against the property if the borrower defaults.

Záložné právo can be created by agreement (zmluvné záložné právo) between the property owner and a lender, or by operation of law (zákonné záložné právo) in certain statutory circumstances. Bank mortgage liens are always contractual and require registration to be effective against third parties.

Before purchasing Slovak property, buyers must inspect Section C of the list vlastníctva to identify any existing záložné právo. A discharge (výmaz) must be arranged from the closing proceeds if the seller has an outstanding mortgage — this should be structured in the zmluva o prevode nehnuteľnosti.

Slovak law also recognises a zábezpeka (security deposit) which differs from záložné právo — it is a contractual retention of funds rather than a registered lien. The two should not be confused: záložné právo is a real right registered against the property; zábezpeka is a personal contractual right against the counterparty.

Common Mistake: Completing a Slovak property purchase without verifying that an existing záložné právo has been properly discharged. A lien registered against a property follows it through sale — the new owner inherits the encumbrance if not removed.
Expert Tip: In the purchase contract, include a condition that the sale price is held in escrow until the seller's záložné právo is formally removed from the Katastrálny úrad. Release funds to the seller only after confirmation of the výmaz.

After full repayment of a Slovak mortgage, the lender must provide a výmaz súhlas (consent to deregistration). Filing this with the Katastrálny úrad removes the záložné právo from Section C of the list vlastníctva, giving the owner a clean title.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between záložné právo and a mortgage in Slovakia?

Záložné právo is the broader legal concept — a registered lien over property securing any type of debt. A Slovak hypotéka (mortgage loan) is the most common commercial product that creates záložné právo, but the lien can also arise from other obligations.

How is záložné právo enforced in Slovakia?

The most common enforcement route is a dražba (public auction) under the voluntary auction act. The lender may also pursue enforcement through a súdny exekútor (court bailiff) via judicial proceedings if contractual enforcement is not available.

How do I remove a záložné právo after repaying a Slovak mortgage?

Request a výmaz súhlas from the lender confirming the debt is cleared, then submit an application for výmaz (deregistration) to the Katastrálny úrad. Once processed, the lien is removed from Section C of the list vlastníctva.
