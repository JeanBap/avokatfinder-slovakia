Kataster Nehnutelnosti (Cadastral Register)

Slovakia's kataster nehnutelnosti is the national register of real property rights, maintained by the Geodesy, Cartography and Cadastre Authority. All property transfers must be registered here to be effective.

The kataster nehnutelnosti (cadastral register of real estate) is Slovakia's unified record of property rights and physical data, maintained by the Urad geodézie, kartografie a katastra (UGKK — Geodesy, Cartography and Cadastre Authority). Every property has a unique parcel number (parcelne cislo) within a cadastral area (katastralne uzemie) and a list of property (list vlastnictva — LV), which records ownership, encumbrances, mortgages, and easements.

The list vlastnictva (LV) is the essential due-diligence document for any Slovak property purchase. It contains three sections: Part A (property description), Part B (ownership and shares), and Part C (encumbrances including mortgages, easements, and restrictions). All three sections must be reviewed before any offer.

The kataster is publicly accessible online at kataster.skgeodesy.sk. Free informative extracts are available online; certified extracts (overene kopie) are available from any UGKK district office or via the Slovak Government portal (slovensko.sk) for a small fee. Banks require certified extracts for mortgage applications.

Slovakia follows a constitutive principle similar to its Czech neighbours: ownership transfers when the registration is completed in the kataster, not when the purchase agreement is signed. The registration application must be submitted within five years of the deed — though in practice it should be submitted immediately.

Registration takes up to 30 days for standard residential transfers. An expedited procedure (urychlenajpostup) of 15 days is available for an additional fee.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Using the informative online extract (not the certified copy) for your due diligence is sufficient for initial checks but banks require the certified overena kopia. Order the certified version as soon as you are serious about a property.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Check Part C of the list vlastnictva carefully for predkupne pravo (pre-emption right) entries — if another party holds a pre-emption right and has not waived it, they can acquire the property at your agreed price within a set period.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the list vlastnictva (LV)?

The property sheet — the official extract from the kataster showing ownership, encumbrances, and property description. Every Slovak property has an LV number. It is the primary title document and must be reviewed before any purchase.

How do I find the LV number for a property?

The estate agent or seller should provide it. Alternatively, search kataster.skgeodesy.sk by address to find the cadastral area and parcel number, which links to the LV.

What does it mean if Part C is empty?

An empty Part C means no encumbrances, mortgages, or restrictions are registered against the property. This is the ideal situation for a buyer — though you should still verify there are no court proceedings affecting the seller personally.
