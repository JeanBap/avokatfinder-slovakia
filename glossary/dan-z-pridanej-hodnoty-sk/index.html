Dan z Pridanej Hodnoty

Dan z pridanej hodnoty (DPH) is Slovak Value Added Tax as applicable to property transactions, particularly the first sale of new residential and commercial buildings by VAT-registered developers, which may attract a reduced or standard rate depending on the property type.

In Slovakia, VAT (dan z pridanej hodnoty, DPH) applies to property transactions when the seller is a VAT-registered economic operator conducting a business — typically a developer building and selling new properties. Private individuals selling their personal homes are not subject to DPH on the sale.

The standard DPH rate in Slovakia is 23% (as of 2025). However, a reduced rate of 5% applies to the supply of social housing — specifically residential buildings and flats with a usable floor area not exceeding 150 m² per flat or 300 m² per family house, subject to conditions set by the VAT Act. Buyers of qualifying new homes benefit significantly from this reduced rate.

The second and subsequent sale of a residential building more than five years after its first occupancy is generally exempt from DPH. This means resales of existing apartments between private sellers typically do not attract VAT. Buyers purchasing from a developer for the first time should confirm whether DPH is included in the stated price.

Commercial property transactions between VAT-registered entities may be structured to allow the buyer to recover input DPH, particularly for properties intended for taxable business activities. The appropriate VAT treatment for commercial purchases should always be reviewed by a Slovak tax adviser.

When DPH applies, it must be clearly stated in the sale contract and, if applicable, the notarial deed. The Slovak tax authority (Financna sprava) monitors developer compliance closely. Buyers who discover post-completion that DPH was incorrectly charged have limited remedies and significant complexity in resolution.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers of new-build flats sometimes assume the advertised price is VAT-inclusive. Developers in Slovakia sometimes quote prices net of DPH. Always ask the developer in writing whether the stated price is inclusive or exclusive of DPH before signing any reservation or purchase agreement.
💡 Expert TipFor residential purchases from a developer, confirm in writing which DPH rate applies and that the price is inclusive of that rate. If the property is between 120–150 m², confirm it still qualifies for the 5% rate under current rules, as eligibility thresholds can change with tax legislation amendments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does DPH apply to purchasing a plot of land?

Building land (stavebny pozemok) sold by a VAT-registered developer is generally subject to DPH. Agricultural land not intended for immediate construction may be exempt.

Can I recover DPH if I am buying for business purposes?

If you are a Slovak DPH-registered company and the property is used for DPH-taxable activities, input DPH may be deductible. Professional tax advice is essential.

Is DPH charged on notary fees in Slovakia?

Yes — notarial services are subject to the standard DPH rate, charged in addition to the regulated notarial tariff.
