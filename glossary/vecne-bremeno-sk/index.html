Vecné Bremeno

Vecne bremeno is an easement or real burden in Slovak property law registered in the Kataster nehnutelnosti that limits how an owner may use their land or grants a specific right over the land to another property or person.

In Slovak civil law (Civil Code, Act 40/1964), a vecne bremeno (easement/real burden) is a registered right that restricts the owner of the burdened property (sluzobna nehnutelnost) for the benefit of either a neighbouring property (panujiuca nehnutelnost) or a specific named individual.

Common vecne bremena include: rights of way (pravo prechodu) over a neighbour's land to reach a public road; utility easements for electricity cables, pipelines, or drainage crossing a plot; and personal rights such as the right to live in a property (pravo dozivotneno byvania) registered in favour of an elderly former owner.

A vecne bremeno is registered in Part B of the List of Ownership (List vlastnictva) in the Kataster nehnutelnosti. Buyers must inspect Part B carefully — a right of way across a development plot or a lifelong residential right registered in favour of a previous owner can dramatically affect the value and utility of the property.

Easements run with the land and are binding on all future owners. They survive a change of ownership and cannot be extinguished simply by sale. Removal requires agreement between the affected parties and cancellation of the Kataster entry, or a court order.

Buyers should pay particular attention to personal easements (osobne bremena), especially rights of residence (byvanie) registered in favour of individuals who may still be alive. Purchasing a property subject to a lifelong residential right means the beneficiary cannot be evicted regardless of the sale.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers sometimes see a vecne bremeno in the List vlastnictva and assume it is a minor formality. A right of residence registered in favour of a living person means they can occupy the property indefinitely — even after the sale. Investigate the nature and holder of any registered vecne bremeno before proceeding.

If so, the property is effectively occupied and the right cannot be easily removed. Negotiate an extinguishment agreement with the beneficiary before purchasing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I buy a property with a vecne bremeno registered against it?

Yes, but you purchase subject to the easement. It binds you as new owner. Assess the impact carefully before deciding whether to proceed and at what price.

How do I find out if a vecne bremeno is registered?

Request a List vlastnictva extract (vypis z katastra nehnutelnosti) from the Kataster portal (katasterportal.sk) or via a licensed Kataster operator. Part B shows all registered burdens.

Can a vecne bremeno be created in a sale contract?

Yes. A seller can reserve a right of residence for themselves or a family member as a condition of sale. The right must be registered in the Kataster to be binding on future buyers.
