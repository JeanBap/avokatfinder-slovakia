Property & Conveyancing

What is Zmluva o prevode nehnuteľnosti (Property Transfer Agreement)?

Slovakia's written property transfer agreement — must have notarised or officially certified signatures and be lodged with the District Office cadastre. An attorney typically prepares and files the application.

Key Facts — Zmluva o prevode nehnuteľnosti (Property Transfer Agreement)
  • Written agreement with notarially authenticated or officially certified signatures required
  • No property transfer tax in Slovakia (abolished 2005)
  • Registration fee: 66 EUR standard (30 days) or 265 EUR expedited (15 days)
  • Non-residents can act via notarised power of attorney
  • Preliminary agreement (zmluva o budúcej zmluve) commonly used before final deed

The zmluva o prevode nehnuteľnosti (property transfer agreement) is the primary legal instrument for real estate transactions in Slovakia under the Občiansky zákonník (Civil Code). It must be in written form and contain a precise description of the property matching its cadastral entry (parcelné číslo, katastrálne územie, and LV number). The agreement becomes legally effective for third parties only upon registration in the Kataster nehnuteľností.

Slovak law requires that the signatures on a property transfer agreement be either notarially authenticated (notárska zápisnica) or officially certified (úradne osvedčené podpisy) by a notary (notár) or by certain authorised officials. A plain private written agreement without certification, while binding between the parties, cannot be registered in the Kataster and thus cannot transfer title. Your Slovak lawyer will arrange signature certification as part of the conveyancing process.

A preliminary agreement (zmluva o budúcej zmluve) is commonly used to lock in a Slovak property transaction while financing, due diligence, or conditions precedent are being satisfied. It creates a binding obligation for both parties to sign the final transfer agreement by a specified date and typically involves a deposit payment. It does not transfer ownership but gives both parties a contractual remedy (including specific performance under Slovak law) if the other party refuses to proceed.

Slovak property transactions incur no national transfer tax — Slovakia abolished real estate transfer tax in 2005, making it one of Europe's most favourable jurisdictions for property acquisition costs. The main costs are: the cadastral registration fee (66 EUR or 265 EUR for expedited), professional fees for the advising attorney, and any notarial fees for signature authentication. Income tax implications for the seller (capital gains) may apply depending on the holding period and nature of the property.

For non-resident buyers, a power of attorney (plnomocenstvo) allowing a Slovak lawyer to act on your behalf at signing and cadastral filing is the most practical solution. The power of attorney must be authenticated — either before a Slovak notary in Slovakia, before a Slovak consulate abroad, or before a foreign notary with an Apostille. Your Slovak lawyer will advise on the appropriate form for your location.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes sign a preliminary agreement and pay a deposit without conducting full title due diligence, then discover an encumbrance or planning issue at the final deed stage. Conduct a full Kataster and planning check before paying any deposit, not just before signing the final agreement.
Expert Tip: Slovakia has no capital gains tax on sale of residential property if you owned and used it as your primary residence for at least 5 years, or if you owned it for more than 5 years regardless of use (with certain conditions). For investment property, different rules apply. Consult a Slovak tax adviser before agreeing a sale price if capital gains implications affect your net proceeds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there stamp duty or transfer tax on property in Slovakia?

No. Slovakia abolished real estate transfer tax in 2005. The only fees on property transfer are the cadastral registration fees (66 EUR standard, 265 EUR expedited) and professional fees. This makes Slovakia one of the lowest-transaction-cost property markets in Central Europe.

What is a zmluva o budúcej zmluve in Slovak property law?

A zmluva o budúcej zmluve (agreement on future contract) is Slovakia's preliminary property agreement — a binding commitment by both parties to sign the final transfer agreement by a set date. It typically includes a deposit payment and conditions precedent. It does not transfer ownership but creates enforceable contractual obligations.

How do I authenticate a power of attorney in Slovakia for a property purchase?

A Slovak power of attorney authorising your lawyer to act on your behalf in a property transaction must be notarially authenticated. You can execute it before a Slovak notary if in Slovakia, before a Slovak consulate/embassy abroad, or before a foreign notary with an Apostille certificate. Your Slovak lawyer will provide the specific wording required.
