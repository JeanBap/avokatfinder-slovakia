What is Eternal Purpose of Ownership?

A concept in Slovak law establishing perpetual use rights of property.

Key Facts — Eternal Purpose of Ownership
  • Recognized in Slovak cadastral law
  • Can be transferred between parties
  • Must be registered officially
  • Protects ownership rights indefinitely
  • Part of EU property standards

This represents a unique aspect of Slovak property law evolved from historical systems.

It defines how property can be held in perpetuity.

Used in cadastral records throughout Slovakia.

Provides legal certainty for long-term holdings.

Requires proper registration in land records.

Common Mistake: Many assume this is the same as full ownership, but it has specific restrictions.
Expert Tip: Always verify the specific terms and conditions when purchasing property with this status.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference from ownership?

Eternal purpose differs as it may have conditions or limitations that don't apply to absolute ownership.

Is it recognized internationally?

It is recognized within the EU framework and Slovak law systems.

Can it be inherited?

Yes, it can be passed to heirs as part of an estate.
