Zmluva o Prevode Nehnutelnosti (Property Transfer Agreement)

Slovakia's zmluva o prevode nehnutelnosti is the written contract transferring property ownership. It must contain specific mandatory elements and signatures must be officially certified (overene podpisy) for kataster registration.

The zmluva o prevode nehnutelnosti (real estate transfer agreement) is the central legal document for Slovak property purchases. Unlike some EU countries, Slovakia does not require a notarial deed for standard residential property transfers — a written agreement with officially certified signatures (urednecertifikované podpisy) is sufficient for kataster registration.

Official certification of signatures can be obtained from a notary (notár), a Slovak Post office (Posta), or any other entity authorised to certify signatures (overovanie podpisov). This makes Slovak property transactions slightly less expensive than full notarial deed countries.

The zmluva must include: precise property identification by parcel number and kataster area; full names and birth numbers (rodne cislo) of parties; the agreed purchase price; a statement that the seller is the legal owner and the property is free of encumbrances (or disclosure of existing ones); and the parties' consent to registration (suhlas so zápisom do katastra).

Before the main purchase agreement is signed, a reservation agreement (rezervacna zmluva) is commonly used, accompanied by a rezervacny poplatok (reservation fee) of 1–3%. This is a private agreement that holds the property while due diligence and financing are arranged. Unlike the main zmluva, it does not transfer any property right.

The zmluva is submitted to the UGKK district office for registration along with a registration application (navrh na vklad), proof of transfer tax payment (darovacia alebo iná listina, alebo doklad o zaplateni nakladu), and the fee. The registration creates the vklad (entry) in the kataster that completes the transfer.

Common Mistake

Watch out: The suhlas so zápisom do katastra (consent to registration) clause is not optional — without it the UGKK will reject the registration application. Ensure your lawyer includes this clause explicitly in every purchase agreement.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Submit the navrh na vklad immediately after signing — Slovak kataster registration is first-come, first-served in terms of priority. A seller who registers a mortgage between your signing and your registration could create complications.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the vklad?

The entry in the kataster that records the transfer of ownership. The vklad is the act that legally completes the transfer under Slovak constitutive principle. Without the vklad, you are not the legal owner.

Can I use a power of attorney for a Slovak property purchase?

Yes. A plnomocenstvo (power of attorney) can be granted to a Slovak lawyer or other representative. For kataster purposes, the POA must be notarially certified — a simple written POA is not accepted for registration submissions.

What is the rodne cislo?

The Slovak birth identification number (similar to a national insurance number) — required for all parties in a property transfer agreement. For foreign nationals without a rodne cislo, a passport number and nationality are used instead.
