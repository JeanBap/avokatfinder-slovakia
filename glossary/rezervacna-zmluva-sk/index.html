Rezervačná Zmluva

A rezervacna zmluva is a reservation agreement in Slovakia under which a buyer pays a reservation deposit to a developer or seller to secure a specific property off the market for a defined period while the full purchase contract is finalised.

In Slovak real estate practice, a rezervacna zmluva (reservation agreement) is commonly used in new-build and resale transactions to secure a buyer's exclusive right to purchase a specific property for a defined period — typically 14 to 60 days — while the parties finalise the purchase contract documentation.

The buyer pays a rezervacny poplatok (reservation fee) — typically 1–3% of the purchase price — which is forfeited if the buyer withdraws without justified reason. If the seller withdraws or fails to deliver the property on the agreed terms, the fee should be returned, though this depends on the specific agreement wording.

A rezervacna zmluva is not a purchase contract and does not transfer any property rights. It is a preliminary commitment that creates an obligation to negotiate and sign the purchase contract (zmluva o prevode vlastnictva) within the reservation period. Buyers should not treat it as equivalent to exchanging contracts.

Because the reservation agreement is not a formal legal act under Slovak civil law, its terms are governed by general contractual principles. Buyers should insist on clear terms: the exact property, the purchase price, the reservation period, the conditions for refund, and the consequences of failure to proceed.

Reservation agreements for new-build developments in Slovakia should be reviewed by a property lawyer before signing. Developers sometimes include clauses that allow price adjustment between reservation and final contract, or that define completion dates vaguely. These provisions can be negotiated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a rezervacna zmluva legally binding in Slovakia?

Yes, as a general contract under Slovak law it is binding between the parties. However, it does not create any registered property right and is not filed with the Kataster.

Can the developer change the price between reservation and final contract?

Only if the agreement permits this. A well-drafted reservation agreement locks the price. Ensure the price is stated as fixed and not subject to adjustment.

Does the reservation deposit count toward the purchase price?

Usually yes — the reservation fee is credited against the final purchase price. Confirm this in writing in the agreement.
